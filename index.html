I'm a Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at The Chinese University of Hong Kong advised by Prof. Ming-Chang YANG. I'm fortunate to work with an advisor who places great trust in my capabilities and encourages me to independently pursue research directions where my passion and curiosity converge.

I'm broadly interested in computer systems, especially operating systems and virtualization. My current research investigates how to leverage heterogeneous memory technologies, including CXL memory, persistent memory, and RDMA, to address critical performance, capacity, scalability, and cost challenges in virtualized cloud infrastructure. My work sits at the intersection of systems software and emerging hardware, exploring novel approaches to memory management that can unlock the full potential of next-generation memory hierarchies.

I earned my Bachelor's degree from Wuhan University in 2021, where I built foundational systems programming skills through projects like toy OS kernels and key-value stores. My internship at Alibaba Cloud, where I contributed to elastic block storage services for large-scale VM deployments, reinforced my passion for systems work that began with PC building and Hackintosh in middle school, experiences that showed me the most interesting problems live in the intricate dance between software and hardware.
